# 20th Century

## The First World War

### Britain before the war
- the early **20th century** was a time of optimism in Britain
- Britain was a global **superpower**
  - large Empire
  - admired navy
  - strong industry
  - strong political institutions
- there was also social progress
  - help for the unemployed
  - old-age pensions
  - free school meals
  - better workplace safety laws
  - tighter town planning to stop slums growing
  - better support for mothers and children after divorce / separation
  - local government became more democratic
  - MPs were paid a salary for the first time

### Outbreak / causes
- **28 June 1914**: **Archduke Franz Ferdinand of Austria** was assassinated
- this triggered the **First World War** (**1914–18**)
- other causes included:
  - nationalism
  - militarism
  - imperialism
  - division of Europe into rival camps

### The war itself
- Britain was part of the **Allied Powers**
  - included **France, Russia, Japan, Belgium, Serbia**
  - later also **Greece, Italy, Romania, USA**
- the Allies fought the **Central Powers**
  - mainly **Germany, Austro-Hungarian Empire, Ottoman Empire**
  - later **Bulgaria**
- the war involved the whole **British Empire**
  - more than **1 million Indians** fought for Britain
  - around **40,000** of them were killed
  - men from the **West Indies, Africa, Australia, New Zealand and Canada** also fought
- millions were killed or wounded
- Britain suffered more than **2 million casualties**
- **July 1916**: **Battle of the Somme**
  - about **60,000 British casualties on the first day alone**

### End of the war
- the First World War ended at **11.00 am on 11 November 1918**
- Britain and its allies were victorious

## Partition of Ireland
- **1913**: British government promised **Home Rule** for Ireland
  - Ireland would have its own parliament but remain part of the UK
- Protestants in the north opposed this and threatened resistance
- the First World War delayed any change
- **1916**: **Easter Rising** in Dublin
  - Irish nationalists rebelled against British rule
  - leaders were executed
- a guerrilla war followed against the British army and police
- **1921**: peace treaty signed
- **1922**: Ireland was divided into two countries
  - **Northern Ireland**
    - six mainly Protestant counties in the north
    - remained part of the UK
  - **Irish Free State**
    - the rest of Ireland
    - later became a republic in **1949**
- some people in both north and south opposed partition
- years of violence followed
- this conflict is often called **the Troubles**

## The inter-war period
- in the **1920s**, living conditions improved for many people
  - better public housing
  - many new homes built
- **1929**: start of the **Great Depression**
- some parts of the UK suffered mass unemployment
- heavy industries like **shipbuilding** were badly hit
- newer industries such as:
  - **automobiles**
  - **aviation**
  grew instead
- as prices fell, many working people had more money to spend
- car ownership rose from **1 million to 2 million** between **1930 and 1939**
- many new houses were built
- it was also a time of cultural growth
  - writers such as **Graham Greene** and **Evelyn Waugh**
  - economist **John Maynard Keynes**
- the **BBC**:
  - began radio broadcasts in **1922**
  - began the world's first regular TV service in **1936**

## The Second World War

### Hitler / outbreak
- **1933**: **Adolf Hitler** came to power in Germany
- he believed the First World War settlement was unfair
- he wanted to expand German territory
- he rebuilt Germany's military and tested its strength in nearby countries
- Britain tried to avoid war
- **1939**: Hitler invaded **Poland**
- Britain and France declared war

### Sides in the war
- the war began between:
  - **Axis powers**
    - Germany
    - Italy
    - Japan
  - **Allies**
    - UK
    - France
    - Poland
    - Australia
    - New Zealand
    - Canada
    - Union of South Africa

### Early war / Churchill / Dunkirk
- after taking **Austria** and **Czechoslovakia**, Hitler also took
  **Belgium** and the **Netherlands**
- **1940**: German forces defeated Allied troops and advanced through France
- at this crisis point, **Winston Churchill** became Prime Minister
- Britain evacuated soldiers from **Dunkirk**
- more than **300,000** men were rescued
- many civilian volunteers used small boats to help
- this became known as the **Dunkirk spirit**

### Britain stands alone / Battle of Britain / Blitz
- from the end of **June 1940** until **June 1941**, Britain and the Empire
  stood almost alone against Nazi Germany
- Hitler wanted to invade Britain but first needed control of the air
- summer **1940**: **Battle of Britain**
  - the RAF defeated the German air campaign
  - key British planes:
    - **Spitfire**
    - **Hurricane**
- Germany then continued bombing British cities at night
- this bombing campaign was called **the Blitz**
- **Coventry** was almost completely destroyed
- there was also major damage in London, especially the **East End**
- British resistance during this period is remembered as the **Blitz spirit**

## Winston Churchill
- **Winston Churchill** (**1874–1965**)
  - son of a politician
  - had been a soldier and journalist
  - became a Conservative MP in **1900**
- became Prime Minister in **May 1940**
- refused to surrender to Nazi Germany
- inspired the British people during the war
- lost the **1945** General Election
- returned as Prime Minister in **1951**
- remained an MP until the **1964** General Election
- after his death in **1965**, he was given a **state funeral**
- in **2002**, the public voted him the **greatest Briton of all time**

### Famous Churchill lines
- **"blood, toil, tears and sweat"**
- **"we shall fight on the beaches ..."**
- **"never ... was so much owed by so many to so few"**

## Later stages of the war
- while Britain defended itself, it also fought elsewhere
- in **Singapore**, Japan defeated British forces
- Japan then occupied **Burma**, threatening **India**
- **December 1941**: Japan attacked **Pearl Harbour**
  - the **USA** entered the war
- in the same year, Hitler invaded the **Soviet Union**
- the Germans were eventually pushed back by the Soviets
- Allied victories in **North Africa** and **Italy** helped turn the war
- American support and German losses in the Soviet Union weakened Hitler

### D-Day / end in Europe / end in Asia
- **6 June 1944**: **D-Day**
  - Allied forces landed in **Normandy**
- after Normandy, the Allies advanced through France and into Germany
- **May 1945**: Germany was defeated
- **August 1945**: war against Japan ended
  - the USA dropped atomic bombs on **Hiroshima** and **Nagasaki**
- the war was over

### Atomic research
- scientists led by **Ernest Rutherford**, working at **Manchester** and later
  **Cambridge**, were the first to **split the atom**
- they also took part in the **Manhattan Project**, which developed the atomic bomb

## Alexander Fleming
- **Alexander Fleming** (**1881–1955**)
  - born in Scotland
  - moved to London
  - became a doctor
- **1928**: discovered **penicillin** while researching influenza
- penicillin was later developed into a usable drug by:
  - **Howard Florey**
  - **Ernst Chain**
- by the **1940s**, it was in mass production
- **1945**: Fleming won the **Nobel Prize in Medicine**
- penicillin is still used against bacterial infections today
