# Values and Principles of the UK

## Core idea
- British society is founded on **fundamental values and principles**
- everyone living in the UK is expected to **respect and support** them
- these values are reflected in the:
  - responsibilities
  - rights
  - privileges
  of being a British citizen or permanent resident
- they are based on:
  - **history**
  - **traditions**
- they are protected by:
  - **law**
  - **customs**
  - **expectations**
- there is **no place** in British society for:
  - **extremism**
  - **intolerance**

## Fundamental principles of British life
- **democracy**
- **the rule of law**
- **individual liberty**
- **tolerance** of people with different faiths and beliefs
- **participation in community life**

## Citizenship pledge
- new citizens make a pledge at the **citizenship ceremony**
- key ideas in the pledge:
  - loyalty to the **United Kingdom**
  - respect for its rights and freedoms
  - uphold democratic values
  - obey the law faithfully
  - fulfil duties and obligations as a British citizen

## Responsibilities of residents / citizens
- **respect and obey the law**
- **respect the rights of others**
  - including their right to their own opinions
- **treat others with fairness**
- **look after yourself and your family**
- **look after the area where you live and the environment**

## Freedoms offered by the UK
- **freedom of belief and religion**
- **freedom of speech**
- **freedom from unfair discrimination**
- **right to a fair trial**
- **right to take part in electing a government**

## Becoming a permanent resident / citizen
- to apply, you need to:
  - **speak and read English**
  - have a **good understanding of life in the UK**

### Two ways to meet the requirement
- **Life in the UK test**
  - questions require English at **ESOL Entry Level 3**
  - usually no separate English test needed for this route
  - people on work visas, including **Tier 1** and **Tier 2**, normally had to pass it
- **ESOL with Citizenship course**
  - for people below **ESOL Entry Level 3**
  - helps improve English and knowledge of life in the UK
  - ends with a test

### After passing
- you can then apply for:
  - **permanent residence**
  - **British citizenship**
- the exact form, evidence and fee depend on personal circumstances

### Later requirement mentioned in the guide
- from **October 2013**, settlement / permanent residence required:
  - passing the **Life in the UK test**
  - **and**
  - acceptable evidence of speaking and listening English at **B1**
- the guide says requirements may change, so current rules should always be checked

## Taking the Life in the UK test
- the test has **24 questions**
- questions are based on **all parts of the handbook**
- questions differ for each person taking the test
- the test is usually taken in **English**
  - special arrangements may be possible in **Welsh** or **Scottish Gaelic**

### Test centres / booking
- the test can only be taken at a **registered and approved** test centre
- there are about **60** test centres in the UK
- booking must be done **online**
- certificates from unregistered centres are **not accepted**
- different arrangements apply for the **Isle of Man** and **Channel Islands**

### On the test day
- read booking instructions carefully
- enter your details correctly
- take:
  - **identification**
  - **proof of address**
- without them, you cannot take the test

## How to use the handbook
- everything needed to pass the test is in the handbook
- questions can come from the **whole book**, including the introduction
- you should study the **entire book thoroughly**
- the language is written for people at **ESOL Entry Level 3 or above**
- the **glossary** at the back explains useful words and phrases
- the **"Check that you understand"** boxes are only guidance
  - they are **not enough on their own**
  - you still need to understand the whole book
