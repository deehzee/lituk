# A Modern, Thriving Society — Religion

## Religion in the UK
- the UK is historically a **Christian** country
- in the **2009 Citizenship Survey**:
  - **70%** identified as **Christian**
  - **4%** as **Muslim**
  - **2%** as **Hindu**
  - **1%** as **Sikh**
  - less than **0.5%** each as **Jewish** or **Buddhist**
  - **2%** followed another religion
  - **21%** said they had **no religion**
- religious buildings for many faiths exist across the UK
  - **mosques**
  - **Hindu temples**
  - **synagogues**
  - **gurdwaras**
  - **Buddhist temples**
- everyone has the legal right to:
  - choose a religion
  - or choose **not** to practise a religion

## Christian Churches

### Church of England
- in **England**, there is a constitutional link between **Church and state**
- the official Church of the state is the **Church of England**
  - also called the **Anglican Church** in other countries
  - called the **Episcopal Church** in Scotland and the United States
- it is a **Protestant** Church
- it has existed since the **Reformation** in the **1530s**

### Monarch / Archbishop / House of Lords
- the **monarch** is the head of the **Church of England**
- the spiritual leader is the **Archbishop of Canterbury**
- the monarch formally appoints the Archbishop and other senior church officials
  - but the choice is usually made by:
    - the **Prime Minister**
    - a Church-appointed committee
- several **Church of England bishops** sit in the **House of Lords**

### Scotland / Wales / Northern Ireland
- in **Scotland**, the national Church is the **Church of Scotland**
  - it is a **Presbyterian** Church
  - governed by **ministers** and **elders**
- the chairperson of the General Assembly of the Church of Scotland is the
  **Moderator**
  - appointed for **one year only**
  - often speaks on behalf of the Church
- there is **no established Church** in:
  - **Wales**
  - **Northern Ireland**

### Other Christian groups
- other Protestant Christian groups in the UK include:
  - **Baptists**
  - **Methodists**
  - **Presbyterians**
  - **Quakers**
- there are also other Christian denominations
- the biggest of these is **Roman Catholic**

## Patron Saints' Days
- each part of the UK has a **patron saint**
- each saint has a special day:

### Wales
- **1 March**: **St David's Day**

### Northern Ireland
- **17 March**: **St Patrick's Day**

### England
- **23 April**: **St George's Day**

### Scotland
- **30 November**: **St Andrew's Day**

## Public holidays / celebrations
- only **Scotland** and **Northern Ireland** have their patron saint's day as an
  official holiday
  - in Scotland, not all businesses and offices close
- events are held across:
  - Scotland
  - Northern Ireland
  - the rest of the UK
- **England** and **Wales** no longer have these days as public holidays
- but they are still celebrated with:
  - parades
  - small festivals
