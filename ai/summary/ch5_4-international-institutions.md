# UK Government, the Law and Your Role — UK International Institutions

## The Commonwealth
- the **Commonwealth** is an association of countries
- members support each other and work together towards shared goals in:
  - **democracy**
  - **development**
- most members were once part of the **British Empire**
- some countries that were not part of the Empire have also joined

### Key facts
- the **monarch** is the ceremonial head of the Commonwealth
  - the text names **King Charles III**
- the Commonwealth has **56 member states**
- membership is **voluntary**
- the Commonwealth has **no direct power** over members
  - but it can **suspend membership**
- it is based on core values of:
  - **democracy**
  - **good government**
  - **rule of law**

## The European Union (EU)
- the **European Union** was originally called the
  **European Economic Community (EEC)**
- it was created by **6 western European countries**:
  - **Belgium**
  - **France**
  - **Germany**
  - **Italy**
  - **Luxembourg**
  - **the Netherlands**
- these countries signed the **Treaty of Rome**
  on **25 March 1957**

### UK and the EU
- the UK first chose **not** to join
- the UK later joined in **1973**
- after the **Brexit** vote, the UK left the EU
- Brexit officially happened at **23:00 GMT on 31 January 2020**
- the text says the EU now has **27 member states**
- it also says that from **2024**, no general principle of EU law is part of UK law

### Types of European law mentioned
- **directives**
- **regulations**
- **framework decisions**

## The Council of Europe
- the **Council of Europe** is **separate from the EU**
- it has **47 member countries**, including the UK
- it works to protect and promote **human rights**
- it **cannot make laws**
- instead, it draws up:
  - **conventions**
  - **charters**

### Best-known convention
- the most famous is the
  **European Convention on Human Rights**
- full name:
  **European Convention on Human Rights and Fundamental Freedoms**

## The United Nations (UN)
- the UK is a member of the **United Nations**
- the UN has **more than 190 member countries**
- it was created after the **Second World War**
- its aim is to:
  - prevent war
  - promote international peace and security

### UN Security Council
- the **UN Security Council** has **15 members**
- it recommends action during international crises and threats to peace
- the UK is one of the **5 permanent members**

## NATO
- the UK is also a member of **NATO**
- **NATO** is a group of **European and North American countries**
- members agree to help each other if one is attacked
- NATO also aims to maintain peace between its members
