# A Modern, Thriving Society — Sport

## Sport in the UK
- sport plays an important part in many people's lives in the UK
- many major events take place at famous stadiums such as:
  - **Wembley Stadium** in London
  - **Millennium Stadium** in Cardiff
- sports facilities are provided by local government and private companies
  - swimming pools
  - tennis courts
  - football pitches
  - dry ski slopes
  - gymnasiums
- many famous sports began in Britain, including:
  - **cricket**
  - **football**
  - **lawn tennis**
  - **golf**
  - **rugby**

## Olympics / Paralympics
- the UK has hosted the **Olympic Games** **3 times**:
  - **1908**
  - **1948**
  - **2012**
- the main Olympic site in **2012** was **Stratford, East London**
- the British team finished **3rd** in the medal table in 2012

### Paralympics
- London also hosted the **2012 Paralympic Games**
- the Paralympics grew out of the work of **Dr Sir Ludwig Guttman**
- he worked at **Stoke Mandeville Hospital** in Buckinghamshire
- he developed new treatment methods for people with spinal injuries
- he also encouraged patients to take part in exercise and sport

## Notable British sportsmen and women
- **Sir Roger Bannister**
  - first man to run a mile in under **4 minutes**
  - achieved this in **1954**
- **Sir Jackie Stewart**
  - Scottish racing driver
  - won the **Formula 1 World Championship** **3 times**
- **Bobby Moore**
  - captain of England's **1966 World Cup** winning team
- **Sir Ian Botham**
  - English cricket captain
  - held many Test cricket batting and bowling records
- **Jayne Torvill** and **Christopher Dean**
  - won Olympic gold in **ice dancing** in **1984**
  - also won **4 consecutive world championships**
- **Sir Steve Redgrave**
  - won rowing gold medals at **5 consecutive Olympic Games**
- **Baroness Tanni-Grey Thompson**
  - Paralympian in a wheelchair
  - won **16 Paralympic medals**, including **11 gold**
  - won the **London Marathon** **6 times**
  - broke **30 world records**
- **Dame Kelly Holmes**
  - won **2 gold medals** in the **2004 Olympics**
- **Dame Ellen MacArthur**
  - became the fastest person to sail around the world single-handed in **2004**
- **Sir Chris Hoy**
  - Scottish cyclist
  - won **6 Olympic golds** and **1 silver**
  - also won **11 world titles**
- **David Weir**
  - wheelchair Paralympian
  - won **6 gold medals**
  - won the **London Marathon** **6 times**
- **Bradley Wiggins**
  - cyclist
  - in **2012**, became the first Briton to win the **Tour de France**
  - won **7 Olympic medals**
- **Mo Farah**
  - British distance runner, born in Somalia
  - won Olympic gold in **2012** for **5,000m** and **10,000m**
  - first Briton to win Olympic gold in the **10,000m**
- **Jessica Ennis**
  - won **2012 Olympic gold** in the **heptathlon**
- **Andy Murray**
  - Scottish tennis player
  - won the **US Open** men's singles in **2012**
  - first British man since **1936** to win a Grand Slam singles title
  - also won Olympic **gold** and **silver** in 2012
- **Ellie Simmonds**
  - Paralympic swimmer
  - won gold medals in **2008** and **2012**
  - youngest member of the British team in **2008**

## Cricket
- **cricket** began in **England**
- it is now played in many countries
- matches can last up to **5 days**
- famous competition:
  - **The Ashes**
    - Test series between **England** and **Australia**
- many cricket expressions are used in everyday English:
  - **rain stopped play**
  - **sticky wicket**
  - **playing a straight bat**
  - **bowled a googly**
  - **it's just not cricket**

## Football
- **football** is the **most popular sport in the UK**
- first professional football clubs were formed in the late **19th century**
- England, Scotland, Wales and Northern Ireland each have separate leagues
- the **English Premier League** has a huge international audience
- many UK clubs also play in European competitions such as the
  **UEFA Champions League**
- most towns and cities have a professional club
- people often strongly support their local team
- there can be strong rivalries between clubs and fans

### National teams
- each UK country has its own national football team
- teams compete in:
  - the **FIFA World Cup**
  - the **UEFA European Championships**
- **England's only major international tournament win** was the
  **1966 World Cup**, hosted in the UK
- football is also widely played at amateur level in parks and local communities

## Rugby
- **rugby** began in **England** in the early **19th century**
- it remains very popular in the UK
- there are **2 types**:
  - **rugby union**
  - **rugby league**
- both have separate leagues and national teams
- Northern Ireland players compete with the **Irish Republic**
- famous competitions:
  - **Six Nations Championship** (rugby union)
  - **Super League** (rugby league club competition)

## Horse-racing
- horse-racing in Britain dates back to **Roman times**
- it has long links with the **Royal Family**
- famous events include:
  - **Royal Ascot**
  - **Grand National** at Aintree
  - **Scottish Grand National** at Ayr
- there is a **National Horseracing Museum** in **Newmarket, Suffolk**

## Golf
- modern **golf** can be traced to **15th-century Scotland**
- played both socially and professionally
- **St Andrews** is known as the **home of golf**
- the **Open Championship** is the only golf **Major** held outside the USA

## Tennis
- modern **tennis** developed in **England** in the late **19th century**
- the first tennis club was founded in **Leamington Spa** in **1872**
- Britain's most famous tournament is **Wimbledon**
- Wimbledon is:
  - the **oldest tennis tournament in the world**
  - the only **Grand Slam** played on **grass**

## Water sports
- **sailing** remains important because of Britain's maritime heritage
- **Sir Francis Chichester**
  - first person to sail single-handed around the world
  - **1966/67**
- **Sir Robin Knox-Johnston**
  - first to do it **without stopping**
- famous sailing event:
  - **Cowes** on the **Isle of Wight**
- **rowing** is also popular
- famous annual event:
  - **Oxford vs Cambridge Boat Race** on the **Thames**

## Motor sports
- motor sport has a long history in the UK
- motor-car racing in the UK began in **1902**
- the UK remains a world leader in motor-sport technology
- a **Formula 1 Grand Prix** is held in the UK every year
- British world champions mentioned in the guide:
  - **Damon Hill**
  - **Lewis Hamilton**
  - **Jensen Button**

## Skiing
- **skiing** is becoming more popular in the UK
- many people ski abroad
- there are also **dry ski slopes** in the UK
- skiing on snow is possible in winter in some places
- there are **5 ski centres in Scotland**
- Europe's longest dry ski slope is near **Edinburgh**
