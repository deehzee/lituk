# UK Government, the Law and Your Role — Your Role in the Community

## Core idea
- becoming a British citizen or settling in the UK brings:
  - **responsibilities**
  - **opportunities**
- everyone has the opportunity to take part in their community
- being active in your community helps make it a better place to live and work

## Values and responsibilities
- shared values and responsibilities in Britain include:
  - **obeying and respecting the law**
  - respecting the **rights of others**
  - treating others with **fairness**
  - behaving **responsibly**
  - helping and protecting your **family**
  - respecting and protecting the **environment**
  - treating everyone **equally**
  - working to provide for yourself and your family
  - **helping others**
  - **voting** in local and national elections
- following these values helps you become a full and active citizen

## Being a good neighbour
- when you move somewhere new, it is good to **introduce yourself** to neighbours
- knowing your neighbours helps you:
  - become part of the community
  - make friends
  - get local help and advice
- good neighbours:
  - respect privacy
  - keep noise down
  - keep gardens tidy
  - only put bins / rubbish out when due for collection

## Getting involved in local activities
- volunteering and helping your community are important parts of good citizenship
- this helps you:
  - integrate
  - meet people
  - support others
  - behave responsibly
  - fulfil your duties as a citizen

## Jury service
- people on the **electoral register** can be chosen at random for **jury service**
- anyone on the electoral register aged **18 to 75** can be asked to serve
- the source notes that the age changed from **70 to 75** in England and Wales
  in **2016**

## Helping in schools
- parents can help at school in many ways:
  - helping in classrooms
  - supporting activities
  - listening to children read
- schools often hold fundraising events for:
  - equipment
  - extra activities
- events may include:
  - book sales
  - toy sales
  - food sales
- many schools have **parent-teacher associations (PTAs)**
- helping with PTA events is a good way to:
  - support the school
  - make friends in the local community

## School governors / school boards
- **school governors** (or **school board members** in Scotland) are people from
  the local community who want to help children’s education
- they must be aged **18 or over**
- there is **no upper age limit**

### Main roles
- **set the strategic direction** of the school
- ensure **accountability**
- monitor and evaluate **school performance**
- you can contact a local school to ask if it needs a new governor / board member

## Supporting political parties
- joining a political party is one way to:
  - support particular views
  - get involved in democracy
- party members are especially active at election time
- they may:
  - hand out leaflets
  - knock on doors
  - ask for support
- this is called **canvassing**
- you do **not** have to tell a canvasser how you plan to vote

### Standing for office
- British citizens can stand for office as:
  - a **local councillor**
  - an **MP**
  - a member of a devolved legislature
  - a **Member of the European Parliament** (as described in the source text)
- some Irish, Commonwealth and certain EU citizens may also be able to stand
  for some offices

## Helping with local services
- people can volunteer with many local service providers, including:
  - hospitals
  - youth projects
  - housing associations
  - museums
  - arts councils
- some organisations also want local people involved in decisions about how
  services are run
- people can volunteer with the police by becoming:
  - a **special constable**
  - a **lay representative**
- people can also apply to become a **magistrate**

## Blood and organ donation

### Blood donation
- donated blood helps people with many injuries and illnesses
- giving blood takes about **1 hour**
- people can register to donate blood

### Organ donation
- many people in the UK are waiting for **organ transplants**
- registering as an organ donor can help your family decide about donation
- living people can also donate a **kidney**

## Other ways to volunteer
- **volunteering** means working for good causes **without payment**
- benefits of volunteering include:
  - meeting new people
  - helping the community
  - practising English
  - developing work skills
  - improving your **CV**
  - helping others

### Examples of volunteer activities
- **working with animals**
- **youth work**
- **helping the environment**
  - e.g. litter pick-ups
- **working with the homeless**
- **mentoring**
  - e.g. helping someone who has left prison
- **work in hospitals / health services**
- **helping older people**

## Charities and voluntary organisations
- the UK has **thousands** of charities and voluntary organisations
- they work for:
  - people
  - animals
  - the environment
- examples mentioned in the text:
  - **British Red Cross**
  - **Age UK**
  - **NSPCC**
  - **Crisis**
  - **Shelter**
  - **Cancer Research UK**
  - **National Trust**
  - **Friends of the Earth**
  - **PDSA**
- charities need volunteers to:
  - help with activities
  - raise money

## Young people and volunteering
- young people can volunteer and also receive accreditation
- one example is the **National Citizen Service**
  - for **16- and 17-year-olds**
  - includes outdoor activities, skills development and a community project

## Looking after the environment
- it is important to **recycle** as much waste as possible
- recycling:
  - uses less energy
  - reduces the need for raw materials
  - reduces landfill waste
- another good way to help your community is to **shop locally**
  - supports local businesses and farmers
  - reduces your **carbon footprint**
- **walking** and using **public transport** also help protect the environment
  because they create less pollution than using a car
