# UK Government, the Law and Your Role — Development of British Democracy

## Democracy
- **democracy** is a system of government where the whole adult population
  gets a say
- this can happen by:
  - **direct voting**
  - choosing **representatives** to make decisions

## Britain at the start of the 19th century
- at the beginning of the **19th century**, Britain was **not a democracy**
  in the modern sense
- there were elections for **Members of Parliament (MPs)**
- but only a small group could vote:
  - **men**
  - aged **over 21**
  - who owned a certain amount of **property**

## Growth of the franchise
- the **franchise** means the number of people who have the right to vote
- during the **19th century**, the franchise gradually expanded
- political parties also began to involve ordinary men and women as members

## The Chartists
- in the **1830s** and **1840s**, the **Chartists** campaigned for reform
- they wanted **6 changes**:

### The 6 Chartist demands
- **every man** to have the vote
- **elections every year**
- **equal regions** in the electoral system
- **secret ballots**
- **any man** to be able to stand as an MP
- **MPs to be paid**

## Later reforms
- at the time, the Chartist movement was mostly seen as a **failure**
- however, by **1918**, most of its demands had been adopted
- voting rights were extended:
  - **1918**: women over **30**
  - **1928**: men and women over **21**
  - **1969**: voting age reduced to **18** for men and women
