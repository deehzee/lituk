# A Modern, Thriving Society — Arts and Culture

## Music
- music is an important part of British culture
- it ranges from **classical music** to **modern pop**
- there are many venues and musical events across the UK

### The Proms
- **The Proms** is an **8-week summer season** of orchestral classical music
- it takes place at several venues, including the **Royal Albert Hall** in London
- organised by the **BBC** since **1927**
- the **Last Night of the Proms** is the best-known concert

### Important classical composers
- **Henry Purcell**
  - organist at **Westminster Abbey**
  - wrote church music, operas and other works
  - helped create a distinct British style
- **George Frideric Handel**
  - German-born, became a British citizen in **1727**
  - wrote **Water Music**
  - wrote **Music for the Royal Fireworks**
  - wrote **Messiah**
- **Gustav Holst**
  - wrote **The Planets**
  - adapted **Jupiter** into the tune for **I Vow to Thee, My Country**
- **Sir Edward Elgar**
  - best known for **Pomp and Circumstance Marches**
  - **Land of Hope and Glory** is played at the Last Night of the Proms
- **Ralph Vaughan Williams**
  - wrote orchestral and choral music
  - influenced by English folk music
- **Sir William Walton**
  - wrote music from film scores to opera
  - best known works include **Façade** and **Balthazar's Feast**
- **Benjamin Britten**
  - famous for operas such as **Peter Grimes** and **Billy Budd**
  - wrote **A Young Person's Guide to the Orchestra**
  - founded the **Aldeburgh Festival** in Suffolk

### Popular music / festivals / awards
- British popular music has had major global influence since the **1960s**
- famous bands include:
  - **The Beatles**
  - **The Rolling Stones**
- later trends included:
  - **punk** in the late **1970s**
  - **boy bands** and **girl bands** in the **1990s**
- major venues include:
  - **Wembley Stadium**
  - **The O2**
  - **SECC** in Glasgow
- major music festivals:
  - **Glastonbury**
  - **Isle of Wight Festival**
  - **V Festival**
- **National Eisteddfod of Wales**
  - annual cultural festival
  - includes music, dance, art and Welsh-language performance
- important music awards:
  - **Mercury Music Prize**
  - **Brit Awards**

## Theatre
- theatres are found in most towns and cities
- they are important to local communities
- **London's West End** / **Theatreland** is especially famous
- **The Mousetrap** by **Agatha Christie**
  - running in the West End since **1952**
  - longest initial run of any show in history

### Musical theatre / pantomime / festivals
- the UK has a strong tradition of **musical theatre**
- **Gilbert and Sullivan**
  - wrote comic operas such as:
    - **HMS Pinafore**
    - **The Pirates of Penzance**
    - **The Mikado**
- **Andrew Lloyd Webber**
  - wrote music for:
    - **Jesus Christ Superstar**
    - **Evita**
    - **Cats**
    - **The Phantom of the Opera**
- **pantomime** is a British tradition
  - often performed at Christmas
  - based on fairy tales
  - light, comic, family-friendly
  - often includes a **Dame** played by a man
- **Edinburgh Festival**
  - held every summer
  - biggest part is the **Edinburgh Festival Fringe**
  - famous for theatre and comedy
- **Laurence Olivier Awards**
  - annual theatre awards in London

## Art
- in the **Middle Ages**, much art in Britain was religious
- after the **Reformation**, wealthy families collected other paintings and sculptures
- many early painters working in Britain were from abroad
  - e.g. **Hans Holbein**, **Sir Anthony Van Dyck**
- British portrait and landscape painters became especially important from the
  **18th century**

### Galleries / Turner Prize
- important galleries include:
  - **The National Gallery**
  - **Tate Britain**
  - **Tate Modern**
  - **National Museum Cardiff**
  - **National Gallery of Scotland**
- **Turner Prize**
  - founded in **1984**
  - awards **contemporary art**
  - one of Europe's most prestigious visual art awards

### Notable British artists
- **Thomas Gainsborough**
  - portrait painter
- **David Allan**
  - Scottish portrait painter
  - famous work: **The Origin of Painting**
- **Joseph Turner**
  - influential landscape painter
- **John Constable**
  - landscape painter
  - famous for **Dedham Vale**
- **Pre-Raphaelites**
  - 19th-century group painting detailed religious and literary scenes
  - included **Holman Hunt**, **Dante Gabriel Rossetti**, **Sir John Millais**
- **Sir John Lavery**
  - Northern Irish portrait painter
- **Henry Moore**
  - sculptor
  - famous for large bronze abstract sculptures
- **John Petts**
  - Welsh artist
  - known for engravings and stained glass
- **Lucian Freud**
  - portrait artist
- **David Hockney**
  - major figure in **pop art**

## Architecture
- the UK has a rich architectural heritage
- important medieval cathedrals include:
  - **Durham**
  - **Lincoln**
  - **Canterbury**
  - **Salisbury**
- the **White Tower** in the **Tower of London** is a Norman castle keep built
  for **William the Conqueror**
- later, rich landowners built great country houses such as **Hardwick Hall**

### Major architects / styles
- **Inigo Jones**
  - designed the **Queen's House** at Greenwich
  - designed the **Banqueting House** in Whitehall
- **Sir Christopher Wren**
  - designed the new **St Paul's Cathedral**
- **Robert Adam**
  - influential Scottish architect
  - designed **Dumfries House**
  - influenced buildings such as the **Royal Crescent** in Bath
- **19th century gothic revival**
  - major buildings included:
    - **Houses of Parliament**
    - **St Pancras Station**
- **Sir Edwin Lutyens**
  - designed **New Delhi**
  - designed war memorials including the **Cenotaph** in Whitehall
- modern architects include:
  - **Sir Norman Foster**
  - **Richard Rogers**
  - **Dame Zaha Hadid**

### Gardens / landscape design
- **Capability Brown**
  - designed natural-looking landscapes around country houses
- **Gertrude Jekyll**
  - designed colourful gardens
  - often worked with Lutyens
- **Chelsea Flower Show**
  - major annual garden design event
