# Britain Since 1945

## The welfare state

### After the war
- although the UK won the **Second World War**, it was economically exhausted
- people wanted major social reform
- wartime changes to education had already raised expectations

### Labour government / welfare state
- **1945**: Britain elected a **Labour government**
- Prime Minister: **Clement Attlee**
- Attlee promised to build the **welfare state** described in the
  **Beveridge Report**
- **1948**: **Aneurin (Nye) Bevan** established the **National Health Service**
  (**NHS**)
  - guaranteed health care for all
  - free at the point of use
- a national system of benefits created **social security**
  - protection from the **"cradle to the grave"**
- the government **nationalised**:
  - railways
  - coal mines
  - gas
  - water
  - electricity supplies

### End of empire / NATO
- another major change was self-government for former colonies
- **1947**: independence granted to **nine countries**
  - including **India, Pakistan, Ceylon (Sri Lanka)**
- other colonies in **Africa, the Caribbean and the Pacific** gained
  independence over the next **20 years**
- the UK also:
  - developed its own **atomic bomb**
  - joined **NATO**
    - alliance against the Soviet threat

### Conservative years in the 1950s
- Conservatives governed from **1951 to 1964**
- the **1950s** were a period of:
  - economic recovery
  - rising prosperity for working people
- **Harold Macmillan** became known for his **"wind of change"** speech
  about decolonisation and independence in the Empire

## Key figures

### Clement Attlee
- **Clement Attlee** (**1883–1967**)
  - born in London
  - became a barrister, then a social worker
  - later a Labour MP
  - had been Churchill's Deputy Prime Minister during the war
- Prime Minister from **1945 to 1951**
- his government:
  - nationalised major industries
  - created the **NHS**
  - strengthened the welfare state
  - improved conditions for workers

### William Beveridge
- **William Beveridge** (**1879–1963**)
  - economist and social reformer
  - best known for the **Beveridge Report** (**1942**)
- the report recommended fighting the five **"Giant Evils"**:
  - **Want**
  - **Disease**
  - **Ignorance**
  - **Squalor**
  - **Idleness**
- it became the basis of the modern welfare state

### R A Butler
- **R A Butler** (**1902–82**)
  - Conservative politician
- responsible for education in **1941**
- oversaw the **Education Act 1944** / **Butler Act**
  - introduced free secondary education in England and Wales
- helped establish the modern split between primary and secondary schools

### Dylan Thomas
- **Dylan Thomas** (**1914–53**)
  - Welsh poet and writer
  - often performed for the **BBC**
- famous works:
  - **Under Milk Wood**
  - **Do Not Go Gentle into That Good Night**
- died in New York aged **39**
- memorials to him are in **Swansea**

## Migration in post-war Britain
- rebuilding Britain after the war created **labour shortages**
- workers were encouraged to come from:
  - **Ireland**
  - other parts of **Europe**
- **1948**: people from the **West Indies** were invited to come and work
- during the **1950s**, labour shortages continued
- more immigration was encouraged for economic reasons
- examples:
  - bus workers recruited from the **West Indies**
  - textile and engineering firms recruited from **India** and **Pakistan**
- for about **25 years**, people from:
  - the **West Indies**
  - **India**
  - **Pakistan**
  - later **Bangladesh**
  came to work and settle in Britain

## Social change in the 1960s
- the **1960s** were a period of major social change
- known as the **"Swinging Sixties"**
- there was growth in:
  - fashion
  - cinema
  - popular music
- famous groups included:
  - **The Beatles**
  - **The Rolling Stones**
- people became better off and bought more:
  - cars
  - consumer goods

### Law / women / technology
- social laws became more liberal
  - including **divorce**
  - **abortion** in England, Wales and Scotland
- women's position at work improved
- Parliament passed laws for:
  - **equal pay**
  - banning discrimination against women because of gender
- technological progress included:
  - **Concorde**
    - developed with France
    - world's only supersonic commercial airliner
- high-rise buildings and concrete / steel architecture became common

### Immigration restrictions
- in the late **1960s**, immigration laws became stricter
- migrants now needed a strong connection to Britain through birth or ancestry
- even so, in the early **1970s**, Britain admitted **28,000 people of Indian
  origin** who had been expelled from **Uganda**

## Great British inventions of the 20th century
- **television**
  - developed by **John Logie Baird**
  - first TV broadcast between London and Glasgow in **1932**
- **radar**
  - developed by **Sir Robert Watson-Watt**
  - first successful test in **1935**
- **Jodrell Bank radio telescope**
  - built by **Sir Bernard Lovell**
- **Turing machine**
  - created by **Alan Turing**
  - key idea behind computer science and modern computers
- **insulin**
  - co-discovered by **John MacLeod**
- **DNA structure**
  - discovered in **1953** through work in London and Cambridge
  - **Francis Crick** was one of the Nobel Prize winners
- **jet engine**
  - developed by **Sir Frank Whittle**
- **hovercraft**
  - invented by **Sir Christopher Cockerell**
- **Concorde**
  - first flew in **1969**
  - began passenger service in **1976**
  - retired in **2003**
- **Harrier jump jet**
  - developed in the UK
- **ATM / cashpoint**
  - invented by **James Goodfellow**
  - first used by Barclays in **Enfield** in **1967**
- **IVF**
  - developed by **Sir Robert Edwards** and **Patrick Steptoe**
  - first test-tube baby born in **Oldham** in **1978**
- **Dolly the sheep**
  - first cloned mammal
  - cloned in **1996** by team led by **Sir Ian Wilmot** and **Keith Campbell**
- **MRI scanner**
  - co-invented by **Sir Peter Mansfield**
- **World Wide Web**
  - invented by **Sir Tim Berners-Lee**
  - first successful web transfer: **25 December 1990**

## Problems in the economy in the 1970s
- in the late **1970s**, the post-war boom ended
- prices of goods and raw materials rose sharply
- the pound became unstable against other currencies
- this caused **balance of payments** problems
  - imports were worth more than exports
- many industries and services were hit by **strikes**
- tensions grew between:
  - **trade unions**
  - the **government**
- many people felt unions had become too powerful

### Northern Ireland unrest
- the **1970s** were also a time of serious unrest in **Northern Ireland**
- **1972**: Northern Ireland Parliament was suspended
- Northern Ireland came under direct rule from the UK government
- about **3,000 people** died in the violence after **1969**

### Mary Peters
- **Mary Peters** (**1939–**)
  - born in Manchester
  - moved to Northern Ireland as a child
- won Olympic gold in the **pentathlon** in **1972**
- later raised money for athletics and promoted sport / tourism
- made a **Dame** in **2000**

## Europe and the Common Market
- **1957**: six countries formed the **European Economic Community (EEC)**
  - West Germany
  - France
  - Belgium
  - Italy
  - Luxembourg
  - the Netherlands
- at first the UK stayed out
- **1973**: the UK joined the **EEC**
- the UK later became part of the **European Union**
- but did **not** adopt the **Euro**

## Conservative government from 1979 to 1997

### Margaret Thatcher
- **Margaret Thatcher** (**1925–2013**)
  - daughter of a grocer from **Grantham**
  - trained as a chemist and lawyer
  - elected Conservative MP in **1959**
  - became Conservative leader in **1975**
- **1979**: became Britain's **first woman Prime Minister**
- served until **1990**
- longest-serving Prime Minister of the **20th century**

### Thatcher era
- Thatcher led major economic reforms
- nationalised industries were **privatised**
- legal controls were placed on **trade unions**
- **deregulation** increased the importance of the **City of London**
- traditional industries such as:
  - shipbuilding
  - coal mining
  declined
- Thatcher worked closely with **Ronald Reagan**
- she welcomed changes in the Soviet Union that helped end the **Cold War**

### Falklands / John Major
- **1982**: **Argentina** invaded the **Falkland Islands**
- Britain sent a naval taskforce and retook the islands
- **John Major** became Prime Minister after Thatcher
- he helped begin the **Northern Ireland peace process**

### Roald Dahl
- **Roald Dahl** (**1916–90**)
  - born in Wales to Norwegian parents
  - served in the **RAF** in the Second World War
- became famous as a writer in the **1940s**
- best-known books include:
  - **Charlie and the Chocolate Factory**
  - **George's Marvellous Medicine**
- several of his books were turned into films

## Labour government from 1997 to 2010
- **1997**: Labour under **Tony Blair** won the election
- Blair's government introduced:
  - a **Scottish Parliament**
  - a **Welsh Assembly**
- Scottish Parliament received strong law-making powers
- Welsh Assembly received fewer legislative powers but much control over
  public services

### Northern Ireland peace process
- Blair built on the Northern Ireland peace process
- **1998**: **Good Friday Agreement**
- **1999**: Northern Ireland Assembly elected
- **2002**: Assembly suspended
- **2007**: Assembly reinstated
- most paramilitary groups later decommissioned weapons and became inactive
- **Gordon Brown** became Prime Minister in **2007**

## Conflicts in Afghanistan and Iraq
- during the **1990s**, Britain played a leading role in:
  - liberation of **Kuwait** after Iraq's invasion in **1990**
  - conflict in the former **Yugoslavia**
- since **2000**, British forces have taken part in operations in:
  - **Afghanistan**
  - **Iraq**
- British combat troops left **Iraq** in **2009**
- in Afghanistan, the UK operated as part of the UN-backed **ISAF** coalition
- the aim was to:
  - prevent Afghanistan being used as a base for terrorism
  - strengthen Afghan security forces
  - support government and development
- the plan was for Afghans to take full security control by the end of **2014**

## Coalition government from 2010 to 2015
- **May 2010**: no party won an overall majority
- first such result since **February 1974**
- Conservatives and Liberal Democrats formed a **coalition**
- **David Cameron** became Prime Minister

## Conservative government from 2015 to 2024
- Conservatives governed from **2015 to 2024**
- Prime ministers during this period:
  - **David Cameron**
  - **Theresa May**
  - **Boris Johnson**
  - **Liz Truss**
  - **Rishi Sunak**
- major events included:
  - **2016 EU referendum (Brexit)**
  - repeated leadership changes
  - the **COVID-19 pandemic**
- Rishi Sunak called a general election for **4 July 2024**
- Conservative popularity declined
- Labour, Liberal Democrats and Greens made gains

## Labour government from 2024
- **2024**: Labour won the general election
- **Keir Starmer** became Prime Minister
- Labour formed the government
