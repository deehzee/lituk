# A Modern, Thriving Society — The UK Today

## Core idea
- the UK today is more **ethnically** and **religiously diverse** than it was
  100 years ago
- because of post-war immigration, nearly **10%** of the population has a
  parent or grandparent born outside the UK
- the UK is a:
  - **multinational** society
  - **multiracial** society
- it has a rich and varied culture

## The nations of the UK
- the UK is in the **north-west of Europe**
- the longest distance on the mainland is from:
  - **John O'Groats** in the north of Scotland
  - to **Land's End** in south-west England
- this is about **870 miles** / **1,400 kilometres**
- most people live in **towns and cities**
- much of Britain is still **countryside**
- people often visit the countryside for:
  - walking
  - camping
  - fishing
  - holidays

## Languages and dialects
- English has many different **accents** and **dialects**
- **Wales**:

## Population growth
- UK population over time:
  - **1600**: just over **4 million**
  - **1700**: **5 million**
  - **1801**: **8 million**
  - **1851**: **20 million**
  - **1901**: **40 million**
  - **1951**: **50 million**
  - **1998**: **57 million**
  - **2005**: just under **60 million**
  - **2010**: just over **62 million**
  - **2022**: estimated **67.6 million**
- population growth has been faster in recent years
- reasons include:
  - **migration into the UK**
  - **longer life expectancy**

## Population distribution
- population is unevenly spread across the UK
- **England**: about **84%**
- **Wales**: about **5%**
- **Scotland**: just over **8%**
- **Northern Ireland**: less than **3%**

## An ageing population
- people in the UK are living longer than before
- reasons:
  - better living standards
  - better health care
- there are record numbers of people aged **85 and over**
- this affects the cost of:
  - **pensions**
  - **health care**

## Ethnic diversity
- the UK is ethnically diverse and changing quickly
- especially in large cities such as **London**
- people in the UK have ethnic origins from all over the world
- in surveys, the most common ethnic description is **white**
  - includes people of European, Australian, Canadian, New Zealand and
    American descent
- other important groups include:
  - **Asian**
  - **black**
  - **mixed** descent

## An equal society
- in UK law, men and women must not be discriminated against because of:
  - **gender**
  - whether they are **married or not**
- men and women have equal rights to:
  - work
  - own property
  - marry
  - divorce
- if married, both parents are equally responsible for their children

### Women and work
- women make up about **half of the workforce**
- on average, girls leave school with better qualifications than boys
- more women than men study at university
- women now work in all sectors of the economy
- there are more women than ever in high-level positions
- men also now work in a wider range of jobs than before

### Family life today
- it is no longer expected that women should stay at home and not work
- many women continue working after having children
- in many families:
  - both partners work
  - both share childcare
  - both share household chores
