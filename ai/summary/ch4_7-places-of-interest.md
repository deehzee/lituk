# A Modern, Thriving Society — Places of Interest

## Countryside / museums / heritage
- the UK has a large network of **public footpaths** in the countryside
- popular outdoor activities include:
  - **mountain biking**
  - **mountaineering**
  - **hill walking**
- there are **15 national parks** in **England, Wales and Scotland**
  - protected countryside
  - open to visitors
  - also places where people live and work
- the UK has many **museums**
  - from small local museums to large national collections
- many famous landmarks are open to the public, usually for a fee

## National Trust
- many places of interest are preserved by:
  - the **National Trust** in **England, Wales and Northern Ireland**
  - the **National Trust for Scotland**
- these are charities that protect:
  - important buildings
  - coastline
  - countryside
- the National Trust was founded in **1895** by **3 volunteers**
- it now has more than **61,000 volunteers**

## UK landmarks

### Big Ben
- **Big Ben** is the nickname of the great bell in the clock at the
  **Houses of Parliament** in London
- many people also call the clock itself Big Ben
- it is over **150 years old**
- the clock tower is officially called **Elizabeth Tower**
  - named in honour of **Queen Elizabeth II's Diamond Jubilee** in **2012**

### Eden Project
- the **Eden Project** is in **Cornwall**
- its **biomes** are giant greenhouse-like structures
- they contain plants from all over the world
- it is also a charity running environmental and social projects

### Edinburgh Castle
- **Edinburgh Castle** dominates the skyline of Edinburgh
- it dates back to the **early Middle Ages**
- it is looked after by **Historic Scotland**

### Giant's Causeway
- the **Giant's Causeway** is on the north-east coast of **Northern Ireland**
- it is a formation of volcanic columns
- it was formed about **50 million years ago**
- many legends are linked to it

### Loch Lomond and the Trossachs
- **Loch Lomond and the Trossachs National Park** is in the west of Scotland
- it covers **720 square miles** / **1,865 square kilometres**
- **Loch Lomond** is the largest area of fresh water in mainland Britain

### London Eye
- the **London Eye** is on the south bank of the **River Thames**
- it is a **Ferris wheel**
- height: **443 feet** / **135 metres**
- it was built for the celebration of the **new millennium**
- it is still an important part of **New Year celebrations**

### Snowdonia
- **Snowdonia** is a national park in **North Wales**
- it covers **838 square miles** / **2,170 square kilometres**
- its best-known landmark is **Snowdon**
  - the highest mountain in Wales

### Tower of London
- the **Tower of London** was first built by **William the Conqueror**
  after **1066**
- visitors can take tours by the **Yeoman Warders** / **Beefeaters**
- the **Crown Jewels** are kept there

### Lake District
- the **Lake District** is **England's largest national park**
- it covers **885 square miles** / **2,292 square kilometres**
- it is famous for:
  - lakes
  - mountains
- popular with:
  - climbers
  - walkers
  - sailors
- the largest stretch of water is **Windermere**
- in **2007**, TV viewers voted **Wastwater** Britain's favourite view
